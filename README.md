# User-Behavior-Analysis
## User Behavior and Marketing Profitability
This project aims to analyze the behavior of users of Showz, an event ticketing platform, in order to optimize the marketing strategy and maximize business profitability. By analyzing data on visits, purchases, and advertising expenditures between 2017 and 2018, the project seeks to understand how users interact with the service, when they decide to buy, how much economic value they contribute over time, and whether the investment in each acquisition channel generates positive returns. With this information, the analytics team can make data-driven decisions about where to invest the marketing budget and which channels to prioritize to acquire profitable customers.

## **Purpose**
Provide the analytics team with data-driven insights to answer:
- How do users interact with the service?
- At what point do they decide to buy?
- How much economic value do they generate over time?
- Which acquisition channels generate profitable customers?

## **Calculated Metrics**
- DAU / WAU / MAU — user activity per day, week and month
- LTV — cumulative customer value by cohort over time
- CAC — acquisition cost per marketing channel
- ROMI — return on advertising investment per source

## **Statistical & Analytical Methods**
- Aggregations by temporal cohort to track behavior over time
- Pivot tables with pivot_table for retention and LTV by life month
- Calculation of normalized retention rates relative to the initial cohort
- User segmentation by profitability (profitable / not_profitable)
- Revenue dispersion analysis by acquisition source
- Identification of the payback point of advertising investment per channel
